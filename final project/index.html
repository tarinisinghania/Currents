WATCHING is an interactive survey that visualizes the psychological effects of digital surveillance through live camera distortion, behavioral inputs, and algorithmic analysis.

The interface translates personal habits, emotional responses, and online behaviors into a speculative surveillance profile. As participants answer questions, the live image becomes increasingly fragmented, scanned, and quantified — reflecting how contemporary systems observe, categorize, and reconstruct identity. The answers calculate a paranoa and fear percentage that results in the visual. Once captured it predicts the personality traits of the person towards the system.

The project explores paranoia, fear and the unstable relationship between humans and machine vision in an era dominated by data extraction and artificial intelligence.
